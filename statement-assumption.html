Implicit Logic Guide

An assumption is Implicit if the person making the statement must believe it to be true for the statement to make sense.

  • The "Bridge" Rule: The assumption connects the premise to the conclusion.
  • Warnings: Assume people want to avoid the penalty or heed the advice.
  • Government Actions: Assume the govt has the authority or resources mentioned.
(A) Only I is implicit
(B) Only II is implicit
(C) Either I or II is implicit
(D) Neither I nor II is implicit
(E) Both I and II are implicit
